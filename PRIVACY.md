# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026_

Claude Usage is a free, open source app that shows you your own Claude usage limits. This policy explains what it does with data. The short version: it collects nothing, and there are no servers.

## We collect no personal data

Claude Usage has no servers and no developer backend. Nothing you do in the app is sent to us or to any third party for analytics, advertising, or tracking. There are no analytics or tracking SDKs in the app.

## How sign in works

You sign in on Anthropic's own page at claude.ai, inside a web view. The app never sees or stores your password. After you sign in, the app keeps only the resulting session token, and only on your device.

## What is stored, and where

- **Your session token** is stored only in this device's Keychain (accessible only when the device is unlocked, and never synced to iCloud or included in backups).
- **Your settings and notification rules** are stored in on device app storage.
- **Your usage history** (the numbers shown in the History view and the activity grid) is stored only in the app's on device container so it can show trends over time.

None of this leaves your device.

## Network

The app communicates only with Anthropic's own domains (claude.ai and api.anthropic.com), over HTTPS, using your own session, to read your own usage. This is the same data you can see on Anthropic's usage page. Anthropic is the data controller for those services; see Anthropic's privacy policy for how they handle it.

## macOS Claude Code stats (optional)

On macOS, if you explicitly grant access, the app can read your local Claude Code logs from your own machine to show your Claude Code usage. This is read only, stays on your device, and you can turn it off at any time.

## Notifications

The app can show local notifications about your usage. These are generated on your device. There is no push server.

## Deleting your data

Choose Sign out in the app to delete the stored session token. Deleting the app removes all of its on device data. There is no server side data to delete.

## Children

The app is general audience and collects no data, including from children.
